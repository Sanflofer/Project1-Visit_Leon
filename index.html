Visit

León is a city in Spain, capital of the province of León and part of the autonomous community of Castile and León, in the northwest of the Iberian Peninsula. León may not be a very well-known Spanish city, but it does offer visitors a fantastic variety of things to see and do. León is home to a wealth of grand, historical buildings, which include some of the most important in Spain.

You cannot leave León without visiting:

Santa Maria de León Cathedral

A gothic jewel from the 13th century and one of the emblems of the city, along with its stained glass windows, it is considered among the best in the world.

St. Isidore's Basilica

One of the most complete pieces of Romanesque architecture. A visit to the Royal Pantheon is essential, which is often refered to as the "Sistine Chapel of Romanesque Art".

MUSAC museum

The MUSAC is one of the architectural jewels of León, which is as interesting on the outside as on the inside. An example of the contemporary art scene.

San Marcos

Architectural landmark of the first order in the Jacobean route, a pilgrimage route taken by christian followers, its construction was born from a donation made by Infanta Doña Sancha, princess and queen of León, in the 12th century.

The Botines Building

At the end of the 19th century, a young architect called Antonio Gaudi, built a unique masterpiece that cities all over the world could only dream of possessing.
